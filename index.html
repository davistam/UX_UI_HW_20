Tolore

Together We Explore

A trip and travel planning app for singles and groups.

Goal

For users to have a less stressful time when planning a group trip—or even a solo trip.

Problem

How might we create a space that helps users get organized when planning trips (group or otherwise) in order to make the experience overall less stressful and more satisfactory for all parties involved?

Solution

To provide organization when planning group trips.

Background

While contemplating the pain points of travel post-pandemic, I came across some recurring themes of frustration. With travel restrictions lifting and borders reopening, people want to get out, plan trips and vacations with friends and family they haven’t seen in person in almost 3 years. They want to create memorable experiences but who wants to plan the trip?

Usually the burden falls on one or two people in the group, everyone struggles to coordinate schedules, budget, find that one article they read last week about things to do at the destination. There is so much to keep track of, it almost seems easier not to go at all. ToLore aims to create a hub that helps mitigate some of the chaos of group planning—though it can easily be used by solo travelers as well.

User Research

1-1 INTERVIEWS
18 Question Survey
12 Participants

I wanted to find out users' motivation to travel, gain insight on their loves, likes, dislikes and loathes, a users' typical travel duration, budget and vacation activities. Effectively, I wanted to gain understanding about the decision-making process and what factors in to having a "good" or "bad" vacation. I used the survey information to inform the persona, creating adventurous Avia,


User Persona

Avia reflected the user's desires to explore, see beautiful vistas, try new foods and enjoys relaxation and adventuring in equal bouts. Users' concerns were the high stress and disorganization when planning a group trip, let alone a solo trip and that they would benefit from a planning tool.

Testing


Everyone struggled to log out. Considering changing the format to the standard hamburger stack or three dots for easier/familiar navigation.

Influencing other changes were things I noticed for possible improvement while the users were testing. For instance the back button on the trip planning homepage was missing, also may be redundant to return to the main profile page after creating the trip.


Iterations

Upon the first round of testing, the feedback I received led me to question the ui writing for the verification screen(users though it would be necessary every time one logs in), users struggled to log out in the buried settings menu so I opted to change to the more familiar hamburger menu. Also to remove the logout button from the home screen. Once I made these iterations in the mid-if quality, I retested and users were able to move through all 4 tasks without issue.


Final Thoughts

From here it would just be continuing to build out the other pages and then proceed with the testing-iteration-loop until we have a really good and solid product that is fun to use and excites people. All apart of enhancing the travel experience.
